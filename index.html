When Automation Breaks: Why Modern Networking Research Needs a New Workflow Paradigm

Imagine running a careful measurement study to understand how an application behaves under different network conditions. Or imagine generating labelled datasets for a supervised learning model, where ground-truth must be collected under tightly controlled environments. Everything is planned. Everything is scripted. Everything works.

Then you try again the next day — and the workflow collapses. A button moves. A new cookie banner appears. A consent dialog loads asynchronously. Automation freezes or fails entirely.

The Hidden Cost of Fragile Automation

Traditional browser automation depends on brittle scripts tied to UI elements. Even minor UI updates break them. Agent-based approaches improve flexibility but introduce latency, cost, and nondeterminism, undermining reproducibility.

Why Data Generation Needs a Rethink

High-quality datasets underpin congestion control evaluation, QoE inference, anomaly detection, and traffic classification. Fragile scripts and manual traces make experiments difficult to reproduce and scale.

Why Application Workflows Are So Hard

Modern workflows involve conditional logic, asynchronous events, pop-ups, and frequently changing interfaces. Automation must adapt without becoming nondeterministic.

How NetGent Works

NetGent separates workflow intent from execution. Researchers define workflows in natural language, which are compiled into abstract NFAs and executed deterministically without runtime AI inference.

Why NetGent Matters

NetGent enables scalable, realistic, and reproducible application traffic generation for networking research, ML training, and system evaluation.

Looking Ahead

NetGent addresses application behavior. Future systems like NetReplica will complement it by modeling diverse network conditions for fully reproducible experimentation.
