Networking Research Automation Challenges

Modern networking research depends on automation for data generation and experimentation, but evolving applications can break traditional workflows.

The Full Research Article

Imagine running a careful measurement study to understand how an application behaves under different network conditions...

This article explores the hidden cost of brittle automation, the challenges of reproducibility, and introduces NetGent — a deterministic, scalable workflow synthesis system designed for modern networking research.

NetGent decouples workflow intent from execution, enabling researchers to regenerate workflows as applications evolve without sacrificing consistency or scale. By synthesizing executable state machines from natural-language workflow definitions, NetGent offers a new paradigm for realistic, repeatable application traffic generation.

This research highlights why automation tooling must evolve alongside applications themselves — and why treating data generation as a first-class system is essential for the future of networking research.
