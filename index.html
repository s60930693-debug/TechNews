When Automation Breaks: Why Modern Networking Research Needs a New Workflow Paradigm

Imagine running a careful measurement study to understand how an application behaves under different network conditions. Or imagine generating labelled datasets for a supervised learning model, where ground-truth must be collected under tightly controlled environments. Everything is planned. Everything is scripted. Everything works.

You log into Instagram. You scroll through several reels. You generate realistic video traffic. You observe how performance shifts as latency increases or bandwidth drops. The experiment runs smoothly. Results look promising.

Then you try again the next day. The login screen changes. A button moves. A consent dialog appears asynchronously. The automation freezes or fails entirely. The workflow collapses — not because the research question changed, but because the application did.

The Hidden Cost of Fragile Automation

This experience is familiar to anyone who has attempted real-world automation for networking research or data generation. Traditional browser automation frameworks depend on brittle scripts tied directly to UI elements. Even minor interface updates are enough to break them.

Agent-based approaches promise flexibility, but they introduce new challenges: high runtime cost, latency from LLM inference, and nondeterminism that undermines reproducibility across experimental runs.

Why Data Generation Needs a Rethink

High-quality data is foundational for congestion control evaluation, Quality of Experience inference, anomaly detection, and traffic classification. Fragile scripts and manually curated traces limit scalability and comparability across research environments.

A New Direction for Workflow Design

A sustainable solution requires separating workflow intent from execution, decoupling logic from UI specifics, and enabling deterministic execution even as applications evolve.
